# Short Squeeze Strategy: Post-Mortem & Success Factors

## 1. The Anatomy of a Squeeze
A Short Squeeze is not an investment; it is a **Liquidity Crisis** for the seller. It happens when the demand for shares (to cover) exceeds the available supply (float), regardless of price.

## 4. Key Success Indicator (The "Checklist")

Before "Sniping a Short", check these 4 pillars:

1.  **High Cost to Borrow (CTB)**: Is it expensive to hold the short? (e.g., CTB > 50%). If CTB is 2%, shorts can wait years.
2.  **No Dilution Risk**: Check recent SEC filings (S-3, 8-K). Is there an active ATM (At-The-Market) offering? If yes, **ABORT**.
3.  **"Not Dead Yet" Catalyst**: The news doesn't have to be great, it just has to be "Not Bankrupt".
4.  **Float Structure**: High Insider Ownership is bullish. It means fewer shares for shorts to grab.

## 5. Retail Strategy
*   **Don't Anticipate**: Wait for the **Technical Breakout** (Price > SMA50 or High Volume Green Candle).
*   **Speed**: Squeezes end violently (volatility collapse). Sell into strength.
